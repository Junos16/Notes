# LInear Systems

Equations are used to model systems mathematically to study their behaviour quantitatively. The equations can involve different kinds of functions, polynomial, exponents, trigonometric, logarithmic, etc. But in most fields it is far more beneficial to model systems using linear equations as much as possible. Even polynomials of relatively lower order are often avoided.

Beyond $x^4$ there is no closed formula for finding solutions to polynomials. And even for quadratics, cubics and quintics, the solutions are extrememly complex. It becomes even more impractical when the number of equations that need to be solved simultaneously for multiple variables is increased.

Linear equations are by far the easiest type of equations to solve. It is far more managable to solve multiple simultaneous linear equations as compared to any other type of equation. Most algorithms used to solve linear equations are of polynomial time complexity. On the other hand, algorithms that are used to approximate solutions to polynomials are of exponential time complexity.
