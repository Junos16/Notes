### Appeal of the Axiomatic Method

Mathematics and Science was developed by studying natural phenomenon and trying to find rules that could best explain those phenomenon. But for mathematicians and scientists, there was no way of knowing whether their explanations corresponded with the true causes of those phenomenon. Nor was there any way for them to know with certainty that there existed such a rule to begin with.

They could try to find other solutions that explain the phenomenon better, but often it is more productive in the short term to work with what they have. To accept the current theory as it is and try to polish it in order to obtain more useful tools for problems they deal with on a day to day basis. 

Example: Even after the development of more advanced theory's of classical mechanics, most of day of day applications of mechanics don't rely on theories like Lagrangian or Hamiltonian Mechanics. Despite having more accurate theories, a large group of physicists and engineers are content with developing and working with Newtonian Mechanics to this day.
